Increase Your Home’s Worth With One Simple Change

Author: Nathan Ilescas

Fri, November 22 at 8pm PST

Did you know that something as simple as bad paint could be costing you thousands of dollars in value?

It’s easy to overlook the impact that faded, chipped, or outdated paint can have, but the reality is, these details can significantly reduce your home's value. The good news? There’s a quick, cost-effective solution that can make all the difference.

How Unappealing Paint Can Tank Your Home’s Value

The exterior paint is one of the first things people notice when approaching a home. If it’s faded, chipped, or peeling, it can give the impression that the house isn’t well-maintained—like seeing a car that's dirty and covered in scratches. Even if everything else is in good condition, a neglected exterior can make people wonder what other issues might be hidden, impacting the home’s overall value.

Inside, poor paint can have a similar effect, making rooms feel smaller or darker than they really are. In a competitive market, these visual drawbacks can lead to lower offers or cause a home to sit on the market longer than you'd want.

Studies show that homes with a neglected appearance—whether from poor paint or other issues—often result in lower sale prices or reduced offers. In fact, homes with exterior issues can sell for 10-20% less than homes in similar condition but with well-maintained finishes.

According to an article on Forbes, homes that are well-maintained, both inside and out, not only attract more buyers but can also command higher prices. Simple improvements, like fresh paint or repaired exteriors, can have a significant impact on how much a home is valued in today’s market

An Easy, Affordable Solution That will Soar Your Home’s Value

So, what’s the simple change that can have a major impact on your home’s value? A fresh coat of paint.

Repainting your home—whether it’s the exterior, interior, or both—can be an affordable way to boost its appeal and increase its perceived value. A well-executed paint job can instantly make your home look cleaner, brighter, and more modern.

  • Exterior Paint

    creates an inviting first impression. A fresh coat of paint on your front door, trim, and shutters can dramatically improve curb appeal.

  • Interior paint

    A fresh coat of paint can make rooms feel larger, more inviting, and more adaptable, helping anyone envision their own style in the space. Neutral tones like light grays, beiges, and whites can brighten up a home and create an open, airy atmosphere, enhancing its overall appeal and making it feel more spacious.

A well-chosen paint color isn’t just cosmetic—it can enhance your home’s key features, making it more desirable and, therefore, more valuable.

The Hidden Value of a Fresh Coat of Paint

You might be wondering—how much of an impact can a simple coat of paint really have on your home’s value?

The answer: quite a bit. Real estate experts agree that a fresh paint job can lead to a significant return on investment (ROI). On average, freshly painted homes can see a 3-5% increase in sale price. For example, if your home is listed at $500,000, a well-executed paint job could increase the sale price by $9,000 to $15,000 or more!

In fact, a recent study by Zillow found that just updating your kitchen with a fresh coat of paint can boost your home’s value by nearly $2,500.

Take Action and Watch Your Home’s Value Rise

Don’t let bad paint hold you back from getting the most out of your home’s value. A simple, well-executed paint job can breathe new life into your property, increase its appeal, and raise its value.

Whether you're considering selling soon or simply want to boost your home's value for the future, a fresh coat of paint is one of the smartest investments you can make.
